Definition

A point is a uniquely identified place in space.

 

A vector   is a directed line segment from point A to point B.

It is directional, and its length can be denoted by |𝑣|

 

Drawing Vectors

NOTE: When drawing vectors, it is important to notate the direction of the line

Simple draw a line between two labelled points, and mark the direction

 

Joining Vectors (Geometrically)

Mark out an origin point.

For each item in the vector, draw a line from the previous point to the new location.

After each item has been drawn, draw another line from the tail of the first line (origin) to the head of the last line.
This newly drawn line is the resultant vector
