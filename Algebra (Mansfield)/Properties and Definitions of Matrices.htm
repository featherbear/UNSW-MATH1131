Matrix Representation:

 

A leading row is a row that is not all zero

 

A leading entry in a leading row is the left-most non-zero value

 

A leading column is a column which contains a leading entry

 

The row-echelon form of a matrix is the term given to when all leading rows are above all non-leading rows.

In every leading row, the leading entry is further to the right than any row higher up.

The reduced row-echelon form of a matrix is one such that every leading entry is one, and that every leading entry is the only non-zero entry in its column.
